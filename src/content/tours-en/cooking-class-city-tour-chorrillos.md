<h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900">Cooking Class</h3>
</div>
<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
<p><strong>Pisco Sour Class:</strong> In this class you will learn everything about pisco, its history and the varieties of this national drink. Afterwards, we will focus on the Pisco Sour, using fresh ingredients: lemon juice, gum syrup, egg white and pisco. Our expert mixologist will guide you so that you can prepare your own cocktail professionally and enjoy its delicious tasting.</p>
<p><strong>Cooking Class (Causa and Ceviche):</strong> We will first delve into the preparation of the <strong>Limeña Cause</strong>. This dish, which combines the softness of mashed yellow potatoes with fresh and colorful ingredients, will be the first culinary challenge. Discover the secrets of its preparation and learn the techniques that make it so special.</p>
<p>Then, we will delve into the preparation of the <strong>Ceviche</strong>. You will learn the ingredients that make up this dish, cutting techniques, lemon marinade, and traditional accompaniments such as sweet potato, corn, and canchita. After cutting the ingredients, we will move on to preparing the garnish and citrus dressings for the iconic Peruvian dish.</p>
<p>Once the preparation is finished, it will be time to enjoy your creations.</p>
</div>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900">Miraflores and Huaca Pucllana</h3>
</div>
<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
<p><strong>Love Park:</strong> We will visit the Parque del Amor located in the famous Miraflores neighborhood. Opened on Valentine's Day in 1993, it is the perfect place to walk, sit on its benches made of colorful mosaics, and read the different phrases of poets dedicated to love.</p>
<p><strong>External View of Huaca Pucllana:</strong> Its panoramic view offers an impressive view of a pre-Inca pyramidal structure that stands out in the middle of the modern city. You will appreciate the unique fusion between pre-Columbian culture and contemporary life.</p>
</div>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900">Historic Center of Lima</h3>
</div>
<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
<p><strong>Plaza Mayor of Lima:</strong> Located in the heart of the historic center and in the same place where Francisco Pizarro founded the city in 1535. Surrounded by some of the most important buildings such as the Cathedral, the Government Palace, the Archbishop's Palace and the Church of the Sagrario.</p>
<p><strong>San Francisco Convent:</strong> Very famous for its catacombs and its incredible library. This monastery also has a basilica where the main altar, the sacristy and the cloister stand out. It is also known for its enormous catacombs where some 70,000 corpses rest.</p>
<p>We finish the tour with a demonstration and tasting of Peru's flagship drink, the “Pisco Sour.” Finally we will board our unit to return to the Hotel.</p>
