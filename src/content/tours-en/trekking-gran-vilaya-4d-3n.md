<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">The Complete Gran Vilaya Expedition</h2>
<p class="text-lg text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">
A challenging and rewarding 4-day expedition through the Gran Vilaya Valley. We will explore the Karajía Sarcophagi before diving into dense cloud forests, ancient trails, and hidden ruins, culminating at the mighty Kuelap Fortress.
</p>
</div>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900">Day 1: Chachapoyas - Karajía - Huaylla Belén</h3>
</div>
<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
<p><strong>08:00 a.m.</strong> Departure from Chachapoyas towards the Cruz Pata community. From here, we walk for about 45 minutes to visit the Karajía Sarcophagi, unique anthropomorphic tombs perched on a cliff.</p>
<p>After the visit and lunch, our transportation takes us to the spectacular Huaylla Belén valley.</p>
<p>We set up our first camp and enjoy dinner surrounded by an impressive landscape.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="bg-white rounded-xl shadow-sm border border-gray-100 p-6">
<div class="flex items-center gap-4 mb-4">
<div class="w-12 h-12 bg-brand-primary text-white rounded-full flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-xl flex-shrink-0">2</div>
<h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900">Day 2: Huaylla Belén - Pirquilla - Congon</h3>
</div>
<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
<p>Following a nutritious breakfast, we start our hike through the cloud forest, observing orchids, bromeliads, and potentially birds like the Andean cock-of-the-rock.</p>
<p>We will hike for approximately 6 to 7 hours, passing through the archaeological remains of Pirquilla, mostly covered by dense vegetation.</p>
<p>We descend to the community of Congon, where we will have dinner and spend the night in a basic hostel or campsite.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="bg-white rounded-xl shadow-sm border border-gray-100 p-6">
<div class="flex items-center gap-4 mb-4">
<div class="w-12 h-12 bg-brand-primary text-white rounded-full flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-xl flex-shrink-0">3</div>
<h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900">Day 3: Congon - Lanche Pass - Yumal</h3>
</div>
<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
<p>Today is a challenging day. We start with a steep ascent for about 4 hours to the Lanche pass, at approximately 3,200 meters above sea level.</p>
<p>The effort is rewarded with panoramic views of the Andes and the Marañón River valley.</p>
<p>After lunch on the trail, we continue our hike to the community of Yumal.</p>
<p>Dinner and overnight stay in a rural hostel in Yumal.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="bg-white rounded-xl shadow-sm border border-gray-100 p-6">
<div class="flex items-center gap-4 mb-4">
<div class="w-12 h-12 bg-brand-primary text-white rounded-full flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-xl flex-shrink-0">4</div>
<h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900">Day 4: Yumal - Kuelap - Chachapoyas</h3>
</div>
<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
<p>We wake up early and hike for about 2 hours to reach the Kuelap Fortress.</p>
<p>Enjoy a guided tour of this impressive archaeological site, built by the Chachapoyas on a mountaintop.</p>
<p>After the visit, we descend to the village of Tingo (by cable car or on foot) for lunch.</p>
<p>Return to Chachapoyas in private transport, arriving in the late afternoon.</p>
</div>
