<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">Gocta Waterfall, Amazonas, Peru</h2>
<p class="text-lg text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">
Join us on an exciting day trip to one of the highest waterfalls in the world at 771 meters, Gocta Waterfall. It consists of two jumps, the first fall of 231 meters corresponds to the entrance through the town of San Pablo de Valera and the fall of 540 meters corresponds to the town of Cocachimba. It is a waterfall that pours over the Amazon Andes. It has been recently discovered and has gained worldwide fame for its scenic beauty.
</p>
<p class="text-lg text-gray-700 leading-relaxed">
The path that leads to the Gocta Waterfall Tour is surrounded by an extensive forest whose vegetation is typical of the cloud forest. Throughout the walk you will enjoy surprising landscapes, you will also be able to appreciate other waterfalls that surround Gocta.
</p>

<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
<p>Our excursion to Gocta begins at <strong>9:00 AM</strong> from Chachapoyas towards the community of Cocachimba. Upon arriving at the town we will visit the Communal Tourism Association to register the visit and receive guidance prior to the walk. We will also make lunch reservations.</p>
<p>The walk lasts five hours round trip (there is the option of renting horses to alternate the journey). This excursion will allow you to appreciate primary forest, orchids, medicinal plants, timber trees, birds such as the Masked Trogon, the endemic Marbled Woodpecker, the Cock-of-the-Rock National Bird, and you can also see animals such as the Yellow-tailed Woolly Monkey among others.</p>
<p>We will also be able to see the daily life of the community, such as their mills for processing sugar cane, and we will be able to see archaeological remains of the Chachapoyas and evidence of geological formations from millions of years ago.</p>
<p>After our visit we return to Cocachimba for lunch. Then we can visit craft centers with the production of textiles, wooden sculptures and ceramics that they offer to the public as souvenirs. Immediately, we began our return trip to the City of Chachapoyas.</p>
