<h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900">El Camotal (The Peruvian Atlantis)</h3>
</div>
<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
<p>Urban legends indicate that, as a result of the earthquake in Lima in 1746, a part of Callao is submerged under the sea, which is a sand bank that can be seen on low tide days and more so during the summer.</p>
</div>
</div>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900">San Lorenzo Island</h3>
</div>
<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
<p>San Lorenzo Island is the largest in the Peruvian sea. Archaeological remains have been found there that indicate that it was formerly used as a religious center. Furthermore, in ancient times, it was occupied by pirates, which gave rise to many fantastic stories and the nickname 'Mysterious Island'. Remains of Chilean soldiers who fought in the Pacific War were also found.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="bg-white rounded-xl shadow-sm border border-gray-100 p-6">
<div class="flex items-center gap-4 mb-4">
<div class="w-12 h-12 bg-brand-primary text-white rounded-full flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-xl flex-shrink-0">5</div>
<h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-900">El Frontón Island (La Cárcel)</h3>
</div>
<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
<p>It is known for having served in colonial times and well into the 20th century as a political prison for figures such as Viceroy Blasco Núñez de Vela, among others. Currently, it is uninhabited.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>
<div class="text-gray-600 space-y-2">
